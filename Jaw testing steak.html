Pan-Seared Steak

Prep Time 15 minutes
Cook Time 10-15 minutes
Serves 2 people

Ingredients

  • Beef steak: 2 premium cuts (ribeye or sirloin), 1-1.5" thick
  • Olive oil: 1 tbsp
  • Butter: 3 tbsp, unsalted
  • Garlic cloves: 2, crushed
  • Fresh thyme or rosemary: 1 sprig
  • Salt & pepper: Kosher salt and freshly ground pepper
  • Optional: Lemon wedge for finishing

Instructions

Prepare

  1. Remove steaks from refrigerator 30-45 minutes before cooking to reach room temperature.
  2. Pat steaks completely dry with paper towels—moisture prevents proper searing.

Season

  1. Season both sides generously with kosher salt and freshly ground pepper just before cooking.
  2. This timing prevents salt from drawing out moisture too early.

Sear

  1. Heat a heavy skillet (cast iron preferred) over high heat until smoking hot.
  2. Add olive oil and swirl to coat the pan evenly.
  3. Place steaks in pan and sear undisturbed for 2-3 minutes to develop a deep brown crust.
  4. Flip steaks and sear the other side for 2-3 minutes.

Baste & Cook

  1. Reduce heat to medium-low and add 2 tbsp butter, crushed garlic, and thyme.
  2. Tilt the pan and continuously spoon melted butter over steaks for 30-60 seconds to baste.
  3. Continue cooking to desired doneness:
    • Medium-rare: 125-128°F (internal temp)
    • Medium: 130-135°F (internal temp)
  4. Remove steaks 5°F below target—carryover cooking will complete the process.

Rest & Serve

  1. Transfer steaks to a warm plate and top each with 1/2 tbsp butter.
  2. Tent loosely with foil and let rest 5-10 minutes—this redistributes juices throughout.
  3. Plate with roasted potatoes or vegetables, drizzle pan juices over steak.
  4. Garnish with fresh herbs and a squeeze of lemon if desired.
