Garlic Bread

Prep Time 10 minutes
Cook Time 12-15 minutes
Serves 4 people

Ingredients

  • Baguette or Italian bread: 1 loaf, halved lengthwise
  • Unsalted butter: 100g, softened
  • Garlic cloves: 6-8, minced
  • Fresh parsley: 3 tbsp, finely chopped
  • Parmesan cheese: 50g, grated (optional)
  • Salt: 1/4 tsp
  • Black pepper: 1/4 tsp, freshly ground
  • Lemon zest: 1/2 tsp (optional but recommended)

Instructions

Prepare the Garlic Butter

  1. In a small bowl, combine softened butter with minced garlic.
  2. Add finely chopped parsley, salt, black pepper, and lemon zest if using.
  3. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly distributed throughout the butter.
  4. Taste and adjust seasonings as needed.

Prepare the Bread

  1. Preheat oven to 190°C (375°F).
  2. Slice the baguette in half lengthwise to create two long, flat pieces.
  3. If using a very thick loaf, you can slice it horizontally into thinner pieces.

Spread the Garlic Butter

  1. Spread the garlic butter mixture generously and evenly over the cut sides of the bread.
  2. Use the back of a knife or spatula to ensure thorough coverage all the way to the edges.
  3. If using parmesan cheese, sprinkle it on top of the buttered bread now.

Toast the Bread

  1. Place bread halves on a baking tray, butter-side up.
  2. Bake for 12-15 minutes until the edges are crispy and golden brown.
  3. The butter should be sizzling and fragrant, and parsley will turn bright green.
  4. Watch carefully—garlic can burn quickly, so check after 10 minutes.

Finishing Touches

  1. Remove from oven and let cool for 1-2 minutes on the baking tray.
  2. Transfer to a cutting board and slice into individual portions (2-3 inches wide).
  3. Serve immediately while hot and crispy.
  4. Garnish with additional fresh parsley if desired for extra color and freshness.

Chef's Tips

  • Quality matters: Use fresh garlic—pre-minced garlic can taste stale.
  • Bread selection: A day-old baguette works better than fresh bread, as it's sturdier.
  • Make ahead: Prepare the garlic butter mixture ahead and refrigerate; spread on bread just before baking.
  • Crispy texture: Broil for the last 1-2 minutes if you prefer extra crispiness, but watch closely to avoid burning.
  • Variations: Add red pepper flakes for heat, sun-dried tomatoes for sweetness, or fresh basil for an Italian twist.
