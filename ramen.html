Homemade Ramen

Prep Time 20 minutes
Cook Time 45-60 minutes
Serves 4 people

Ingredients

For the Broth

  • Chicken or vegetable stock: 1.5 liters (6 cups)
  • Soy sauce: 3 tbsp
  • Mirin: 2 tbsp
  • Ginger: 2 tbsp, grated
  • Garlic cloves: 4, minced
  • Green onions: 2, sliced
  • Mushrooms: 200g (shiitake or cremini), sliced
  • Salt & pepper: To taste

For the Noodles & Toppings

  • Ramen noodles: 400g fresh or dried
  • Soft-boiled eggs: 4, halved
  • Rotisserie chicken: 200g, shredded (or protein of choice)
  • Bean sprouts: 100g
  • Bamboo shoots: 100g (canned is fine)
  • Nori (seaweed): 4 sheets, cut into strips
  • Sesame seeds: For garnish

Instructions

Prepare the Broth

  1. Heat stock in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, simmer for 5 minutes to infuse flavors.
  3. Stir in soy sauce and mirin until combined.
  4. Add sliced mushrooms and cook for 8-10 minutes until tender.
  5. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.
  6. Keep broth warm on low heat while preparing other components.

Soft-Boil the Eggs

  1. Bring a separate pot of water to boil.
  2. Carefully add eggs and cook for 6-7 minutes for a jammy, runny yolk.
  3. Transfer to ice bath and let cool for 2-3 minutes.
  4. Peel gently under cool running water.
  5. Cut eggs in half just before serving.

Cook the Noodles

  1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add ramen noodles and cook according to package directions (usually 3-5 minutes for fresh, 8 minutes for dried).
  3. Drain and rinse noodles briefly with hot water to prevent sticking.

Prepare Toppings

  1. Heat bean sprouts and bamboo shoots in the broth for 1-2 minutes.
  2. Warm shredded chicken separately in a small amount of broth if desired.
  3. Arrange all toppings on a plate for easy assembly.

Assemble & Serve

  1. Place cooked noodles into 4 serving bowls.
  2. Ladle hot broth over noodles, filling bowls about three-quarters full.
  3. Arrange toppings: place egg halves on top, then add chicken, bean sprouts, bamboo shoots, and nori strips.
  4. Drizzle with sesame oil if desired and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  5. Garnish with sliced green onions and additional sesame seeds.
  6. Serve immediately while hot and enjoy!
